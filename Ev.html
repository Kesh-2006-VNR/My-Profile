Self-Driving Electric Vechicle

Description

This project presents an innovative smart vehicle capable of maintaining its balance autonomously and navigating without human input. Engineered with a dual-wheel vertical design and a central horizontal stabilizing wheel, the vehicle achieves dynamic self-balancing using real-time sensor feedback (IMU/MPU6050). The self-driving functionality is powered by intelligent algorithms that allow the vehicle to detect obstacles, plan paths, and make navigation decisions without external control. The system is built using a microcontroller platform (such as Arduino or ESP32) and may integrate components like motor drivers, ultrasonic sensors, and gyroscopes. By combining mechanical stability, autonomous navigation, and real-time processing, this project demonstrates the future of intelligent mobility — one that requires no hands, no steering, and no compromise on control.

Purpose

This project is a self-balancing and self-driving smart vehicle designed specifically to empower individuals with physical disabilities. By eliminating the need for manual control or balance, the vehicle offers safe, stable, and independent mobility to those who may struggle with conventional transportation methods. With its intelligent self-balancing mechanism and autonomous navigation system, the vehicle ensures smooth travel on various terrains — all without the need for hands or assistance. It’s not just a machine; it’s a step toward inclusive innovation, aiming to restore independence and dignity through engineering.

Features & Highlights

Self-Balancing Mechanism

  • Uses real-time sensor feedback (like MPU6050/IMU) to maintain upright balance.
  • Eliminates the risk of tipping — perfect for users who can't support the vehicle manually.

Autonomous Driving

  • Navigates on its own using obstacle detection and path planning.
  • No need for steering or throttle control — ideal for users with limited mobility.

Designed for the Differently-Abled

  • Created specifically to help handicapped individuals move independently.
  • Restores confidence and freedom with smart, safe mobility.

Smart Sensor Integration

  • Equipped with IMU, ultrasonic sensors, and motor control logic.
  • Ensures safe navigation even in narrow or uneven paths.

Energy Efficient

  • Powered by lightweight batteries with optimized motor control for long-lasting operation.
  • Compact and low-power — easy to use indoors and outdoors.
Kinesthetic Bot

Description

This project introduces a revolutionary Kinesthetic Bot — a smart, gesture-controlled robotic assistant that responds intuitively to full-body movements. Designed with advanced motion tracking using a webcam and MediaPipe Pose, the bot interprets human gestures in real-time to perform tasks, move in desired directions, or interact with its environment. Integrated with a microcontroller platform like Arduino and powered by motor drivers, the system offers seamless physical interaction without the need for joysticks, remotes, or voice commands. Whether used for control, assistance, or education, this bot exemplifies the next level in human-machine interaction — where your body becomes the controller.

Purpose

The Kinesthetic Bot is built to empower users through intuitive, touchless control. Especially beneficial for individuals with speech impairments or upper limb challenges, this system replaces conventional input methods with natural full-body gestures. By merging accessibility with robotics, it offers a hands-free interface for interacting with intelligent systems — bridging the gap between physical limitations and digital control. It’s not just about control, but about expression, independence, and inclusion through motion.

Features & Highlights

Gesture-Based Control

  • Uses full-body motion tracking via webcam and MediaPipe Pose.
  • Responds to specific gestures like arm movement, leg shift, or posture to drive actions.

Arduino/Microcontroller Integration

  • Built on platforms like Arduino or ESP32 for real-time motor actuation and sensor interfacing.
  • Highly customizable and extendable.

Motor-Driven Movement

  • Moves in response to gestures — forward, backward, turn, stop, or custom tasks.
  • Can be connected to wheels, arms, or robotic platforms.

AI Feedback Loop

  • Captures screen or task context and uses AI (e.g., ChatGPT) for dynamic responses.
  • Enables intelligent help or suggestions based on what the user is doing.

Assistive & Inclusive

  • Designed with accessibility in mind.
  • Suitable for users with speech impairments or limited upper-limb mobility.
  • Encourages independence and confidence through body-powered interaction.

Modular & Expandable

  • Can be enhanced with tactile sensors, voice alerts, or object recognition.
  • Future-ready architecture for use in education, healthcare, or personal robotics.
